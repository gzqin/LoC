    • NEWS

      June 2018

      Zhenzhen Qin is offered a permanent faculty position on the Assistant Prof. level at Zhengzhou University, China, where Guangzhao Qin got his B.S. degree in 2011.

      June 2018

      Guangzhao Qin obtains the Ph.D. degree from RWTH Aachen University, Germany after 3 years of study supervised by Prof. Ming Hu. The overall grade of SUMMA CUM LAUDE is awarded.

      May 2018

      The first paper from the CMP Lab @LoC is published in Nanoscale, which is entitled "Origin of anisotropic negative Poisson's ratio in graphene". [Nanoscale 10, 10365 (2018)]

      March 2018

      Guangzhao Qin is honored as the Outstanding reviewer for the journal of “Computational Condensed Matter”.

      February​ 2018

      We receive the congratulations from The Royal Society of Chemistry: ‘Diverse anisotropy of phonon transport in two-dimensional group IV-VI compounds: A comparative study’ [Nanoscale 8, 11306 (2016)] is one of the top 100 cited articles published in Nanoscale in 2016 based on Web of Science citations from January 2018.

      December 2017

      Guangzhao Qin is honored the National Award for Outstanding Self-financed Chinese Students Abroad 2017.

      November 2017

      Zhenzhen Qin gets the AICES fellowship awarded by the Aachen Institute for Advanced Study in Computational Engineering Science (AICES), RWTH Aachen University, Germany. She will conduct the Post-doc research in cooperation with Prof. Bob Svendsen and Prof. Ming Hu.

      July 2017

      Zhenzhen Qin obtains the Ph.D. degree from Nankai University, China under the supervision of Prof. Xu Zuo. She is the 2017 Outstanding Graduates from Nankai University.

    • LAB OPENINGS

      Currently, the lab is only hosted at Zhengzhou University, China.

      • Undergraduate Research Opportunities: We are looking for highly motivated undergraduate students to join our lab. Being an interdisciplinary group, we have projects for students with different background and interests. Undergraduate students are also encouraged to publish their research results. Computational programming skill and English would be strong plus.
      • Graduate students with a background in mathematics, mechanical engineering (especially heat transfer), materials science, electrical engineering, physics, chemistry or related fields are welcome to get in touch. Prior experience in the related areas is NOT a must. Computational programming skills would be strong plus.
      • Postdoc: We currently do not have postdoc openings. Interested postdoc candidates can get in touch, and we will contact you once new positions are available.
      • Visiting scholars are welcome to contact Dr. Zhenzhen Qin to discuss projects of mutual interest.
